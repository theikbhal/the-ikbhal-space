7 Rules Billionaires Follow (But Never Discuss)

Based on 60 years of watching smart people destroy themselves.

01

Learn from Others' Mistakes

Study market crashes and bankruptcies. "I'm not smart enough to make every mistake myself."

02

Don't Buy What You Don't Get

If you can't explain it to a 10-year-old, don't own it. Avoid flashy crypto or complex derivatives if you don't master them.

03

Stop Being in a Hurry

Wealth is built in the sitting and waiting, not the trading. 20% compounding for decades is the "secret."

04

Extreme Concentration

Diversification is protection against ignorance. If you know what you're doing, you only need 6-10 great businesses.

05

Watch Fees & Taxes

Minimize trading to avoid capital gains. Taxes and fees are the slow leak that sinks the ship.

06

Master Your Emotions

The market is a machine to transfer money from the impatient to the patient. Fear and greed are your only true enemies.

07

Never Quit Early

The magic happens at the end of the curve. Most people quit right before the exponential phase kicks in.

Don't Buy a Car: The Wealth Destroyer

Cars are depreciating assets that consume capital, insurance, maintenance, and interest. For the first few decades of compounding, every dollar wasted on a status symbol is a dollar removed from your future billion-dollar legacy.
