Why Mike Chose Siem Reap

While Phnom Penh offers the amenities of a major capital, it comes with the stress of one. Siem Reap was chosen specifically for:


1. Walkable Infrastructure: A rare gem in SE Asia. New sidewalks allow you to walk from the city center to the temples safely.

2. Less Traffic: You don't spend your retirement stuck in a car. The flow is relaxed.

3. Vibe Profile: It feels like a permanent vacation spot rather than a concrete jungle. The river, the trees, and the low-rise buildings create a peaceful atmosphere.

The Tourist/Retiree Overlap

Because Siem Reap hosts Angkor Wat, it punches above its weight. It has the restaurants, international airport, and services of a big city, but packaged in a small, manageable town size. In Phnom Penh, you might struggle to find English speakers in local areas. In Siem Reap, the tourism industry ensures English is widely understood.
